Right‑Align the Unit, then “Replace 1 with 20”

This shows parents the exact technique: write the unit under the big number and right‑align it. Then visually replace the 1 in 10,000 with 20—meaning “20 ten‑thousands make two‑hundred‑thousand.”

Number
200,000
Unit (right‑aligned)
10,000
Replace 1 → 20 because 20 × 10,000 = 200,000
20 ten‑thousands = 200,000

All digits are right‑aligned so place values line up exactly.

Also works for “hundreds”
200,000 ÷ 100 = 2,000 hundreds
…and “tens”
120,000 ÷ 10 = 12,000 tens
…and “thousands”
320,000 ÷ 1,000 = 320 thousands
Parent script
“Put the unit under the number, right‑align it, then replace the 1 with the count that makes the top number.”
